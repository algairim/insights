`2026 June 2`

When the machine's hour costs almost nothing, the human hour needs a reason. That is the quiet question underneath the productivity story. The [Phantom Effort](disclaimer.md) concept catches the hollow version — the machine produces the work while the human performs the effort. The [Price of a Human Hour](disclaimer.md) concept asks it straight: when the machine's hour is free, what is yours worth, and worth for what? A business that bills by time has to answer directly, because the [Unbillable Hour](disclaimer.md) concept is exact — once the overhead is automated, you have to decide what the time is for.

There is a harder edge. Some of the dull work was never just dull. The [Dignity of the Dull Task](disclaimer.md) concept holds that some drudgery is how care gets expressed, and automating it can hollow the thing it served. The owner who does the books by hand because it keeps them close to the business is not being inefficient — they are paying attention. The task to automate is the one that is only cost. The task to keep is the one that was also contact. Knowing the difference is the whole skill now.
