# The questions to ask before you buy AI

`2026 June 3`

Most AI purchases are decided by a demo and a discount, which is exactly backwards. The [vendor due-diligence study](disclaimer.md) lays out the questions that actually matter before signing: where does your data go, who can see it, what happens to it after the contract ends, what is the real accuracy on your kind of work rather than the vendor's curated demo, and what does it cost when usage triples. None of these come up in a sales meeting unless you raise them.

The other half of the decision is whether to buy at all. The [build-versus-buy analysis](disclaimer.md) shows why first-year cost is a misleading guide: buying looks cheaper until the subscription compounds and the workflow is locked to a vendor's roadmap, while building looks expensive until you count the maintenance no one staffed. And the [total-cost-of-ownership view](disclaimer.md) is where most business cases quietly fail, because the licence is the small number. Integration, training, the human who checks the output, and the cost of being wrong are the large ones.

For anyone advising a business through this, the value is not knowing which tool is best. It is knowing which questions the buyer would not have thought to ask.
