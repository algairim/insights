# Professional Services AI

`2026 May 31`

Professional services — law, accountancy, architecture, consulting — hit AI critical mass in early 2026. The signal is not adoption numbers but commercial commitments. [KPMG deployed Claude to 276,000 staff](disclaimer.md) across all divisions. [Enter, Brazil's first legal AI unicorn, reached billion-dollar valuation](disclaimer.md) using an 80-million lawsuit backlog as its moat. AI tools covering [audit sampling, tax research, and client communications for accounting firms](disclaimer.md), and [contract review, precedent search, and client intake for legal practices](disclaimer.md), are commercially available and being deployed at scale. Large-firm adoption has set client expectations that will cascade to mid-market and small-firm equivalents within 24 months.

The specific pressure on small professional service firms is the expectation gap. When a client's large-firm counterpart processes documents in minutes with AI, the firm that takes days looks slow rather than careful. [A solicitor case study](disclaimer.md) in this project shows the pattern: a firm that advises on orderly process running on the post. [An accountancy practice](disclaimer.md) that tells clients to go digital while still waiting for the shoebox. These are not firms unaware of AI. They are firms that have not yet found the entry point that fits their structure — which is usually a single document-heavy process that currently requires reading and routing by a senior person. The value in professional services AI lands first in the document-heavy, compliance-tracking work that surrounds the core professional judgment, not in the judgment itself. The judgment stays human. The paper infrastructure around it is where the time goes.
