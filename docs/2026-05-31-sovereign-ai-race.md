# The Sovereign AI Race

`2026 May 31`

The geopolitical story running underneath the AI capability race through 2025–2026 is the competition for sovereign AI: the ambition of nations to control their own models, infrastructure, and data rather than depend entirely on US hyperscalers. [Japan committed $6 billion to sovereign AI](disclaimer.md) and its major industrial corporations formed a trillion-parameter AI joint venture. [Saudi Arabia declared 2026 the Year of Artificial Intelligence](disclaimer.md), with AMD, Cisco, and HUMAIN forming a 1GW AI infrastructure joint venture. [India's IndiaAI Mission 2.0](disclaimer.md) framed a "UPI for AI" national stack for its 60 million MSME sector. [France and Germany handed public administration AI](disclaimer.md) to Mistral and SAP via sovereign cloud. [Cohere absorbed Aleph Alpha at $20 billion](disclaimer.md) to create a European challenger.

The logic driving every programme is the same: whoever controls the training data, the model weights, and the inference infrastructure controls the intelligence layer of the national economy. Governments routing all public-sector AI through US providers are creating a dependency as strategic as single-supplier energy dependence. [The Pax Silica semiconductor alliance](disclaimer.md) demonstrated that hardware access itself is being weaponised as a geopolitical tool. Countries outside the alliance face compute access risk. The response across Europe, the Gulf, India, and Asia has been to build indigenous capability fast enough to matter before the dependencies become structural.

For a consulting practice, the sovereign AI race has a practical dimension: it creates national AI programmes that fund SME adoption. [Singapore's NAIIP funds 10,000 firms](disclaimer.md). [Maharashtra offers a 20% subsidy for 5,000 MSMEs](disclaimer.md). [Poland's AI Systems Act created a free regulatory sandbox for small firms](disclaimer.md). The UK's [£500 million Sovereign AI Fund](disclaimer.md) and [Scotland's £8.2 billion AI Growth Zone](disclaimer.md) illustrate the pattern at European scale. Sovereign AI programmes generate downstream SME adoption budgets that create advisory-accessible work.
