`2026 June 2`

The early AI conversation was about coders, marketers, and call centres.

The 2026 reality reaches a great deal further down. There are now working playbooks for [pet grooming and boarding](disclaimer.md), for [personal trainers writing programmes and chasing check-ins](disclaimer.md), for [the sports club run by one volunteer with a spreadsheet](disclaimer.md), for [language schools still building the September timetable by hand](disclaimer.md), for [the trades that run on whiteboards](disclaimer.md).

None of these are tech businesses. Most assumed AI was for someone else. What the work actually lands on is the same in every case: the admin around the craft, never the craft itself. The groomer still grooms; the trainer still trains. [Where professional services hit critical mass first](2026-05-31-professional-services-ai.md), the long tail of hands-on, owner-run businesses is following — quietly, one back office at a time. The question is no longer which sectors. It is which ones noticed.
