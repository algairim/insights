`2026 June 9`

Watch where the money is moving and a pattern appears: the leading AI companies have decided that the model is no longer the hard part. Getting it to work inside a real organisation is. So they are buying and building the layer that does exactly that. One frontier lab [formed a $1.5bn enterprise services firm with two of the largest names in finance](disclaimer.md). Another [acquired an Edinburgh consultancy outright](disclaimer.md) to get the people who deploy. Software vendors now [put their own engineers inside every agent rollout](disclaimer.md), and the model is spreading internationally — [Japan's firms are signing the same forward-deployed arrangement](disclaimer.md).

This is a confession dressed as a strategy. The industry has learned that [the demo that worked rarely reaches production](disclaimer.md), and that the distance between the two is filled with human work — understanding the process, cleaning the data, wiring the systems, changing how people actually do the job. Capability is abundant. Deployment is scarce.

For anyone advising businesses, this is the whole opportunity stated plainly by the people who make the models. The value was never in having access to intelligence. Everyone has that now. The value is in the unglamorous mile between a working tool and a changed business — and that mile is now the product.
