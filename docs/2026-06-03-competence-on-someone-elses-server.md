# When your competence lives on someone else's server

`2026 June 3`

There is a trade buried in every AI subscription that almost no one prices. The [subscription to yourself](disclaimer.md) concept names it precisely: when the thing that makes you effective runs on a server someone else controls, your competence can be repriced, throttled, or revoked, and there is nothing you can do about it on the day it happens.

This is not paranoia. It is the structure. The [disposable expert](disclaimer.md) concept describes expertise rented by the sentence, available instantly and gone the moment the bill lapses. The [model landscape is concentrating](disclaimer.md) around a small number of providers, and billing is [moving to consumption](disclaimer.md), which means the price of your capability is now set by someone whose interests are not yours. A business that has rebuilt its workflow around one vendor's tool has not bought a capability. It has rented one, on terms it does not control.

The answer is not to refuse the tools. They are too useful, and the leverage is real. The answer is to know which parts of the business you have quietly made dependent on a service that can change overnight, and to keep at least one thing — the data, the skill, the fallback — that is still yours when the terms change. They always change.
