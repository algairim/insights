Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is currently the most practical technology for giving a language model access to a specific business's own information — without training a bespoke model or paying for fine-tuning. The architecture is straightforward: documents, records, and business knowledge are stored as searchable embeddings in a vector database; when a user asks a question, relevant chunks are retrieved and inserted into the model's context alongside the query; the model answers using both its training knowledge and the retrieved material. For an SME, this means a general-purpose model like Claude or GPT can effectively answer questions about the firm's own invoices, contracts, policies, product catalogue, or customer history — without the business's data ever being used to train the model. [The AI Memory Layer concept](disclaimer.md) is the client-facing description of what RAG enables: AI that permanently knows the business, remembers its decisions, and can surface relevant context faster than any human search.

The practical SME applications are immediate. A letting agency whose compliance calendar lives in a spreadsheet can build a RAG system that answers "which properties have a gas safety check due in the next 30 days" without rebuilding the spreadsheet. An accountancy firm whose advice archive lives in email can surface relevant past work when a similar client question arrives. A hospitality operator whose seasonal playbook lives in the owner's head can transcribe it and make it searchable. The entry cost is low: [n8n](disclaimer.md) or similar no-code automation platforms can wire a document store to a language model with no custom code.

The limitations matter as much as the capabilities. [RAG evaluation research](disclaimer.md) shows that retrieval quality — whether the right chunks are surfaced — is the dominant determinant of output quality, and retrieval quality depends almost entirely on data quality upstream. A RAG system built on poorly structured, inconsistently formatted, or outdated internal documents will produce unreliable outputs regardless of the model tier. [Advanced RAG techniques](disclaimer.md) such as Self-RAG and RAG-Fusion address retrieval failures but cannot substitute for clean source data. The first question in any knowledge system engagement is therefore whether the data is in a state where retrieval is even possible — and if not, what the remediation path is. That question is often where the real consulting work begins.
