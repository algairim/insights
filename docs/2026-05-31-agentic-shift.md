`2026 May 31`

The shift underway in AI is from systems you talk to, to systems that act on your behalf. An AI agent does not respond to a prompt and wait — it perceives its environment, plans across multiple steps, executes actions (code, API calls, web searches, file writes), and adapts on feedback without a human approving each move. [The state of the field in 2026](disclaimer.md) is that the capability is real, deployment is accelerating, and the failure modes are poorly understood by most of the organisations adopting them. The most important research findings of 2026 are not about capability. They are about failure.

The enterprise picture shows how far and how fast this has moved. [Workday reported](disclaimer.md) that agentic AI contract value was up 200% year-on-year, with 4,000 customers now running agents. [Salesforce's Agentforce Operations](disclaimer.md) targets back-office exception handling and claims 70% cycle-time reductions. [SAP declared the "Autonomous Enterprise"](disclaimer.md), framing ERP functions as a crew of collaborating agents. The direction of travel for enterprise software is clear: every major platform is becoming an agent orchestrator. The companies that control which agents run on which data, and under what governance, are the ones controlling the operating model of the businesses they serve.

For SMEs the agentic shift lands differently. The enterprise platforms are calibrated for organisations with Salesforce or SAP as their system of record. An SME running Xero, Pipedrive, and Google Workspace cannot buy Agentforce Operations and expect it to work. The same agentic capability has to be assembled from five vendors instead of purchased from one, and the integration is the customer's responsibility. [Anthropic's Agent SDK](disclaimer.md) and Claude for Small Business with pre-built SaaS connectors are attempting to close this gap from the model-provider side, but the structural challenge remains: the SME that wants Fortune 500 back-office intelligence in 2026 has to either find an integration partner or wait for the market to consolidate enough to offer it as a packaged product.
