# The Model Is Not the Bottleneck

`2026 May 31`

At its May 2026 Code with Claude developer conference, [Anthropic did not release a new flagship model](disclaimer.md). What it shipped was an orchestration layer sitting on top of its existing models: managed agents, scheduled routines, remote agent execution, and an advisor pattern in which a cheap executor model escalates to a more capable one only on the hard cases. The company building the world's most capable models stated explicitly that the marginal value of the next model is now smaller than the marginal value of the scaffolding that decides which model runs, when, against which trigger, and with what escalation path. Three years of model races trained organisations to defer action until the next better model arrived. Anthropic just said: stop waiting.

[The IBM operating model research](disclaimer.md) arrived at the same conclusion from the deployment side: the organisations that scaled AI did not do it by finding a better model. They did it by answering four questions about a specific process and owning the outcome. The consultant whose value proposition is access to AI tools or knowledge of the latest model releases is already a commodity — those things are available to anyone with a browser. The consultant who sits with a firm, identifies the specific recurring process worth automating, connects the trigger to the routine to the escalation path, and measures whether the output changed is doing the work Anthropic just declared to be the frontier. The intelligence is available to everyone. The wiring is the job.
