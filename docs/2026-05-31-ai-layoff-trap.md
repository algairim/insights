`2026 May 31`

Approximately 80% of enterprises surveyed by Gartner in [its May 2026 survey of 350 global executives](disclaimer.md) had reduced workforce tied to AI adoption. The ROI improvement was identical to the organisations that did not cut. The layoffs created budget headroom. They did not create returns. Gartner's own analyst named the mechanism: the organisations that improved AI ROI were the ones that invested more aggressively in skills, roles, and operating models — not the ones that eliminated headcount. The cost reduction was treated as a proxy for transformation. It is not.

The specific data is instructive. [Cloudflare cut 20% of staff in May 2026](disclaimer.md), named the agentic AI era as the cause, reported 34% revenue growth year-on-year, and saw its stock fall 24% on the news. [Morgan Stanley found](disclaimer.md) that UK and US firms deployed AI in the same functions and reported the same 11.5% productivity gains. UK firms converted the gain into headcount cuts. US firms converted it into capacity expansion. Same technology, different management decision about what to do with the productivity benefit — and the UK firms ended the period with an intact efficiency claim and a depleted institutional memory.
