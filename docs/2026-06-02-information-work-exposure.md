`2026 June 2`

The question stopped being whether AI displaces work. It became which work.

A 2026 study put [93% of information-work occupations at moderate displacement risk by 2030](disclaimer.md) — the desk jobs, the ones built on reading, writing, summarising, reconciling. Not the trades. Not the hands. The keyboard.

This is the pattern one country already lived. A $42bn industry was [built on tasks AI can now do](disclaimer.md), and the lesson was not mass disappearance but a change of altitude — the work that survives moves up, toward judgement, exception-handling, the cases the script doesn't cover. [That earlier read on the shift](2026-05-31-philippines-lesson.md) still holds.

For anyone running a business, the implication is uncomfortable in a specific way. The roles most exposed are often the ones that feel safest — salaried, indoors, professional. The work hardest to automate is frequently the work that was hardest to hire for: the person who knows why the exception happened. One forecast modelled [a net 600,000 jobs lost in a single economy](disclaimer.md) under a moderate scenario, white-collar first. The number to watch is not the headcount. It's the altitude.
