# The money says AI is not a fad you can wait out

`2026 June 3`

A reasonable instinct for a cautious business owner is to wait. Hype cycles pass; the prudent move is often to let the dust settle and adopt the survivors. The scale of capital now committed makes that instinct, in this case, a mistake.

A leading lab has [filed confidentially for a public offering at close to a trillion-dollar valuation](disclaimer.md), having [crossed $45bn in annual recurring revenue](disclaimer.md). One analyst house puts [global AI spending at $2.59 trillion this year](disclaimer.md). A single hardware maker is [shipping more AI servers than personal computers, with a $51bn backlog](disclaimer.md). These are not the numbers of a passing trend. They are the numbers of an infrastructure build-out, the kind that happened with electricity and broadband, and the kind that does not get unwound.

The practical reading is not that any particular tool will last. Many won't. It is that the capability itself is now permanent furniture, and a business deciding whether to engage is no longer betting on whether AI sticks around. It is only choosing when it starts learning to use what is already here to stay.
