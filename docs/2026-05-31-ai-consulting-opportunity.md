# The AI Consulting Opportunity

`2026 May 31`

The consulting opportunity in AI for SMEs is not a prediction about a future market. It is a documented gap in the current one. [YouGov's August 2025 polling of UK SME leaders](disclaimer.md) found that 26% of non-adopting SME owners would likely hire external consultants to support AI implementation. Applied to the UK's approximately 5.5 million SMEs, even a conservative reading represents a market of significant scale. The same data found that 60% of UK businesses cite limited AI skills as the primary barrier — not cost, not willingness, not relevance to their sector. The demand is present. The skilled supply is not. The consulting value in this market is specific: not access to AI models, which every owner can acquire independently, and not general AI awareness, which two years of vendor content has delivered abundantly. It is someone who sits with the specific business, understands the specific bottleneck, identifies the specific process worth automating, and stays until it works differently.

[Spain adds a second signal](disclaimer.md): 24% of Spanish SMEs name digitisation and automation as their top challenge, Spain leads Europe on this measure, and the fear signal — "we know we must act and can't get it to stick" — is the precise profile of a business that will seek outside help. The pattern holds across markets: demand is documented, skilled supply is scarce, and the need is specific enough that generic vendor content does not meet it. The gap between an organisation that has decided to move and one that has actually changed how work moves is where the practical value sits.
