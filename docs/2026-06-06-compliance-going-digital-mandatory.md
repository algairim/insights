# Compliance Is Going Digital-Mandatory

`2026 June 6`

A quiet shift is happening at the boring end of running a business: the paperwork is becoming legally required to be digital. The [study of e-invoicing mandates](disclaimer.md) lays out the direction — jurisdiction after jurisdiction is moving to make structured electronic invoicing compulsory, and once it is mandatory, manual handling stops being a choice and becomes a liability. The same logic is spreading to [export documentation](disclaimer.md), where the rules are fixed and repetitive enough that automation handles them well, and to [food traceability](disclaimer.md), where proving provenance on demand is increasingly the price of selling at all.

For the businesses whose entire value rests on origin, this lands hard. A [protected saffron whose worth is its provenance](disclaimer.md) keeps that record in disconnected folders; a [protected paprika producing thousands of tonnes a year](disclaimer.md) tracks plot-to-tin in a notebook. The certification that lets them charge a premium is exactly what the regulator will eventually ask them to produce in a structured, auditable form. The notebook is not just inefficient; it is becoming non-compliant.

The reframing for a small firm is that this is not a tech project to defer. It is a deadline that arrives whether or not the business is ready, and the cheapest moment to digitise a record is before someone with statutory power asks to see it.
