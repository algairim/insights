`2026 June 2`

Every trust mechanism we rely on assumed forgery was expensive.

A handwritten signature, a crowd, a recorded voice, a photograph — each worked as proof because faking it cost more than it was worth. That assumption is gone. The [Manufactured Grassroots](disclaimer.md) concept names it directly: when a crowd costs nothing to fake, numbers stop proving anything. The [Synthetic Past](disclaimer.md) concept extends it to records — any document forgeable or plausibly deniable. The [Cost of a Signal](disclaimer.md) concept makes the general rule plain: when proof of care becomes free, it stops being proof. And the [Proof of Human](disclaimer.md) concept asks the question underneath all of them — what proves a human was involved at all.

For a business this is concrete and immediate. The voice on the phone authorising a payment, the email from the supplier changing bank details, the document that looks exactly right — none can be trusted at face value any more. [The voice and the face can be faked; the callback that stops the fraud cannot](disclaimer.md). The defence is no longer better detection of the fake. It is a process that does not depend on the signal being real — a second channel, a callback, a step that cannot be generated. Trust has to move from the artefact to the procedure.
