`2026 June 3`

The pricing model under AI tools is quietly shifting from a seat to a meter, and it changes how a business should budget. A widely used coding assistant has [moved every plan to usage-based billing](disclaimer.md), and a major model provider has [split its billing so that automated agents draw from their own credit pool](disclaimer.md). The old question was how many licences you need. The new question is how much the work consumes, and that number rises with exactly the success you were hoping for.

This matters most for anything agentic, because an agent that runs on its own can consume far more than a person clicking buttons. A workflow that costs little in a pilot can cost a great deal once it is doing real volume, and the [total-cost-of-ownership view](disclaimer.md) is where that surprise usually lands. The right instinct is not to fear it but to instrument it: know the cost per task before you scale the number of tasks.

Priced by use, AI rewards the businesses that know what each automated action is worth. The ones that don't will discover the meter the way everyone discovers a meter. At the end of the month.
